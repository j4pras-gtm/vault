# Profile Intelligence

This module encodes the patterns found in the profiles of top LinkedIn creators (based on an analysis of 425 profiles, focusing on those with the highest follower counts and engagement).

## 1. Headline Formulas

Top creators do not use their job title as their headline. They use their headline as a value proposition and positioning statement.

### The "I Help" / Outcome-Driven Formula
**Structure:** "I help [Target Audience] achieve [Specific Outcome] through [Mechanism]."
**Examples:**
- "I help B2B AI products go to market and build repeatable systems to get customers."
- "I help founders and gtm operators create markets, not fight for them 🏴‍☠️"
- "I help you get paid to post on LinkedIn"

### The Multi-Identity / Credibility Stacker
**Structure:** [Current Role] | [Past Impressive Role] | [Key Achievement] | [Topic they post about]
**Examples:**
- "3× Co-Founder | CEO @ Musemind GmbH | UX Design Awards Jury | Top #2 Design Leadership Voice 🇩🇪"
- "Sales Trainer & SKO Speaker | USA Today Bestselling Author | Sales Strategist for Orgs That Outbound"
- "Bestselling Author | Bringing My Go-To-Market Method to 10K Orgs | B2B AI GTM Consultant"

### The Niche Authority / Category Creator
**Structure:** Claiming ownership over a highly specific, emerging category.
**Examples:**
- "I teach AI Agents and Lead Gen | Lead Gen Man(than) | 100K+ students"
- "Founder at LeadMagic | Building API-first AI enrichment, workflows, and GTM infrastructure"
- "Your go-to for GTM, AI & Lead gen!"

## 2. About Section Frameworks

The "About" section is rarely a standard resume summary. It functions as a sales page or a manifesto.

### The Origin Story Opening
**Pattern:** Start with a dramatic, humble, or highly specific moment from the past to build empathy before selling.
**Examples:**
- "I started my career sleeping on concrete bricks. I got paid pennies for 15 hours of work, seven days a week."
- "I decided to skip the traditional corporate path & start building in the AI x Sales space at 27."
- "I started FullEnrich after a career in sales + a first company in the sales tech space."

### The Manifesto / Belief Statement
**Pattern:** Open with a strong, contrarian, or philosophical belief about the industry.
**Examples:**
- "The loudest don't win. The most resonant do."
- "Momentum favors the people willing to rethink, move first, and figure it out in motion."
- "They don’t call me early. They call me when something already broke."

### The Direct Pitch
**Pattern:** Immediately state what the company does, who it is for, and the scale at which it operates.
**Examples:**
- "Henry Schuck is the founder and CEO of ZoomInfo, (NASDAQ:GTM) a Go-To-Market Intelligence Solution for more than 35,000 companies worldwide."
- "We help B2B saas companies build multichannel acquisition systems!"

## 3. Positioning Strategies

- **The "Builder in Public":** Positioning oneself not as an untouchable expert, but as a peer who is figuring things out and sharing the notes. (e.g., "Building (in public) the first autonomous business...")
- **The "Operator, not Guru":** Emphasizing that they actually do the work, rather than just talking about it. (e.g., "Tomorrow’s GTM Systems, Built for you")
- **The "Category Bridge":** Connecting an established field with an emerging one to capture search intent. (e.g., "AI x Sales", "GTM Engineer")

## 4. Authority Building Patterns

- **Metric Dropping:** Embedding hard numbers directly into the headline or first line of the summary (e.g., "$6M ARR", "100K+ students", "48k+ followers").
- **Brand Association:** Mentioning recognizable brands or platforms (e.g., "USA Today Bestselling Author", "TEDx Speaker", "Ex – Telenor Group").
- **The "Contact Me" Gate:** Using the first line of the summary purely for inbound routing (e.g., "For brand collaboration: email@domain.com"), which signals high demand.
