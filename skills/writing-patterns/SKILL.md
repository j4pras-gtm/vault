# Writing Patterns Intelligence

This skill module encodes the structural writing patterns extracted from a dataset of 3,121 high-performing LinkedIn posts.

## 1. Hook Frameworks

Based on engagement data, hooks fall into distinct psychological categories.

### The Contrarian / Myth-Buster
**Pattern:** State a widely accepted belief, then immediately destroy it.
**Why it works:** Creates cognitive dissonance and forces the reader to stop scrolling to resolve it.
**Examples:**
- "You don't need to pay an agency $1000s to get a list of leads who are ready to buy."
- "Everyone's rushing to build AI agents, but 90% are just building glorified chatbots."
- "Being the smartest in the room is a liability."

### The Number / Stat Drop
**Pattern:** Open with a highly specific, often large or unusual number.
**Why it works:** Specificity builds instant credibility. It signals "I have real data, not just opinions."
**Examples:**
- "We just spent $64,362 on completing the largest cold email deliverability test we have ever done."
- "7 out of 10 businesses are missing the AI automation opportunity."
- "months ago, lead-gen was 'the main problem', now they book 85 meetings p/m + $500'000 in the pipeline"

### The Personal Story Arc
**Pattern:** Open with a vulnerable, dramatic, or highly personal statement about a past event.
**Why it works:** Humans are wired for narrative. It builds immediate empathy and curiosity.
**Examples:**
- "I was fired for being too honest."
- "I once had a teammate who did way less activity than me."
- "I hate switching between ChatGPT and Claude because it forget my entire project context!"

### The Direct Call-Out (Audience Filter)
**Pattern:** Name the exact target audience in the first line.
**Why it works:** Acts as a dog-whistle for the ideal reader while filtering out noise.
**Examples:**
- "GTM Engineers & Agency Owners:"
- "SaaS Founders & Agency Owners:"
- "Hey Sales Cold Emailers,"

## 2. Post Structures

High-performing posts follow predictable architectural flows.

### The Bullet List Framework (35% of high-engagement posts)
1. **Hook:** Bold claim or promise of value.
2. **Context:** 1-2 lines explaining *why* this matters now.
3. **The List:** 5-7 bullet points (often using emojis like ↳, 👉, or ✅).
4. **The Turn:** A concluding thought that ties the list together.
5. **CTA:** Ask for engagement or offer a resource.

### The Personal Narrative Arc (20% of high-engagement posts)
1. **The Incident:** A dramatic opening statement ("I was fired...").
2. **The Struggle:** 3-4 lines detailing the pain or challenge.
3. **The Epiphany:** The moment of realization or the pivot.
4. **The Resolution:** Where things stand now (the success).
5. **The Lesson:** 2-3 bullet points summarizing the takeaway for the reader.
6. **Soft CTA:** A reflective question to the audience.

### The Lead Magnet Giveaway (Highly viral)
1. **The Flex:** "I just built/compiled [Valuable Resource]."
2. **The Proof:** "It took me [X hours/dollars] to make."
3. **The Tease:** 3 bullet points of what's inside.
4. **The Gate (CTA):** "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll DM it to you. (Must be connected)."

## 3. CTA Types

### The Engagement Gate (Highest volume of comments)
- **Structure:** "Comment [WORD] and I'll send you [Resource]."
- **Requirement:** Often paired with "Make sure we're connected so I can DM you."

### The Soft Reflective Ask
- **Structure:** "What's your take on [Topic]?" or "Over to you: [Specific Question]?"
- **Purpose:** Drives thoughtful, longer comments which algorithmically boost the post.

### The Authority / Funnel Push
- **Structure:** "If you want to build [Outcome], check out the link in my featured section."
- **Purpose:** Direct conversion, usually placed after a highly educational "How-To" post.

## 4. Flow Patterns

- **The 1-3-1 Rhythm:** One short sentence. A paragraph of three sentences. One short sentence. Creates visual breathing room.
- **The "Broetry" Line Break:** Single sentences separated by double line breaks. Used heavily in the "Personal Narrative Arc" to force a slower reading pace.
- **The Emoji Anchor:** Using specific emojis (🚨, ♻️, ↳) not for decoration, but as visual bullet points to guide the eye down the page.
